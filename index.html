About Lighta

Here's A Little About Me

I began my musical journey at a very young age. Always tapping my feet or beating on a surface. Music also runs in my bloodline, having a large family full of talented musicians/singers of all sorts. I was basically born into the world of music.

I began learning music from various teachers. Nicole Brown, Anthony McQuay, Elizabeth Thornton, and Sacario Leadon, just to name a few. I also must give props to Marvin Sands for teaching me how to perform on stage and how to be versatile with my music. He was a big inspiration in my musical life.

My first musical instrument was the piano. I would always recommend the piano to all beginners interested in learning. It teaches you flexibility with both hands, and also how to read sheet music at a high level. I also learnt the trumpet and I began learning the bass guitar a while back. I hope to learn as many instruments as I can and further my musical knowledge. I also began songwriting in my adolescent years. I have wrote songs for myself and also assisting many local other artists with their lyricism.

For my performance aspect, I have performed at local bars and lounges. I have also performed at a few competitions such as The Music Project and the E. Clement Bethel National Arts Festival of 2018. I was named the national winner of the Sr. High rap category at the National Arts Festival. I was awarded a plaque in remembrance of my achievement. In terms of my production aspect, I have self-produced all of my songs since 2020. I have released a lot of singles and I have also released a mix tape and two albums recently. I firmly believe in self-production because it taught me independence in my craft, and also not having to wait on anyone to give me the okay for my own music. I know a lot of talented producers and engineers, but I preach to others that nothing is better than knowing you own 100% of your craft.

I see my musical productions becoming global entities. I would like my music to reach the ears of billions, and I pray that it has a positive outreach on every single person. I see my music changing the hearts and minds of many, and influencing young artists to do the same. God spares life, you are looking at a global artist before his time.
